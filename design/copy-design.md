## Copy Position

Legacy should sound like a small, serious atelier: direct, exacting, cinematic, and human. The copy should make the studio feel premium without sounding distant. It should make the team feel creative without becoming vague.

Primary positioning sentence:

> Legacy Sound Design is a boutique audio atelier creating focused, immersive sound experiences for film, video, brands, production, and themed environments.

Shorter version:

> Focused, immersive sound design for stories, spaces, brands, and experiences.

Plain version:

> Legacy Sound Design creates immersive sound for film, video, brand work, live production, and themed experiences.

## Voice Rules

Use:

- Precise, cinematic language.
- Concrete nouns: room, scene, cue, system, story, audience, environment, mix, detail.
- Active verbs: shape, build, tune, design, guide, support, finish.
- Human scale: direct collaboration, close attention, one-on-one process.
- Future-aware language while the project archive is still growing.

Typography note:

- Use Newsreader for atelier/prestige moments: hero lines, page titles, pull quotes, and short statements that should feel enduring.
- Use Space Grotesk for clarity and forward vision: body copy, CTAs, service explanations, process copy, and client-facing instructions.
- Use Space Mono only for metadata, specs, archive labels, and technical details.

Avoid:

- Empty luxury language.
- Generic agency phrases like "full-service solutions" or "elevate your brand."
- Overly technical audio jargon on top-level pages.
- Claims about awards, client scale, or finished case studies until confirmed.
- Saying "we have done" for work that is still upcoming.

Preferred tonal pairings:

- Premium, not cold.
- Artistic, not vague.
- Technical, not inaccessible.
- Boutique, not tiny.
- Ambitious, not inflated.

## Messaging Pillars

### Focused, Immersive Sound Design

Core idea:

Legacy shapes sound around story, space, and audience attention. The work should feel intentional at every scale, from a single sonic cue to the full emotional arc of an environment.

Use this copy:

> We design focused, immersive sound experiences that help a world feel built, inhabited, and alive.

Alternate:

> Every cue has a job: pull the audience closer, clarify the moment, and make the world feel more complete.

### One-On-One Client Relationship

Core idea:

The boutique model means clients work directly with the people making the sound. No account layers. No anonymous handoff. Clear communication from first call to final delivery.

Use this copy:

> Our process is direct and collaborative. You work one-on-one with the people designing, shaping, and finishing the sound.

Alternate:

> We keep the relationship close: clear scope, direct conversation, careful listening, and a team that stays connected to the creative intent.

### The Guys Behind The Magic

Core idea:

The team should feel approachable and capable. Use "the guys behind the magic" as a warm section idea, but keep the actual copy polished.

Use this section title:

> The team behind the magic

Use this copy:

> Legacy is built by three makers with overlapping practices in sound, systems, performance, and production. Small team, close collaboration, careful work.

Alternate:

> Meet the people shaping the signal: artists, technologists, and problem-solvers working behind the scenes to make each project feel alive.

## Process Copy

Use a simple four-step process. Keep it grounded and client-friendly.

### 1. Listen

```text
We start with the world of the project: story, space, audience, references, constraints, and what the sound needs to make possible.
```

### 2. Shape

```text
We build a focused sonic direction, then test it against the emotion, pacing, and practical needs of the work.
```

### 3. Refine

```text
We stay close through feedback, revisions, and technical realities, keeping the work clear without sanding off its character.
```

### 4. Deliver

```text
We prepare final materials with the right format, context, and handoff for the production, platform, or space.
```

Compact process copy:

```text
Listen closely. Shape the world. Refine the details. Deliver with care.
```

## Copy QA Checklist

Before publishing copy, confirm:

- The page says atelier or boutique in a meaningful way.
- The services include film/video, brands, production, and themed experience.
- The copy does not imply published Legacy case studies before they exist.
- The client relationship is clearly direct and one-on-one.
- Team bios only use facts that can be supported by public profiles or direct approval.
- CTAs feel conversational, not salesy.
- No generic audio cliches have slipped in.
- The copy sounds like the same brand described in `design/visual-identity.md`.
